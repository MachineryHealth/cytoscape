Rintaro Saito
Assistant professor of Keio University. Major scientific field is bioinformatics. I was born in Tokyo, Japan in 1972. In my early childhood, I lived in Canada from 1983 to 1986. During my master and doctoral period in Keio University, I computationally analyzed sequence patterns of translation initiation sites and proposed a model of molecular evolution, indicating that AUG triplets are depleted around start codons to avoid spurious translation initiation.
    After getting my Ph.D, I was appointed to a research post in RIKEN, the institute of physical and chemical research and developed a computational method to eliminate false positive data from protein-protein interaction network.
    In 2002, I returned to Keio University as an assistant professor and worked on non-coding RNA analyses. Although knowledge of non-coding RNA was limited at that time, I showed that there are many non-coding RNAs in various species including prokaryotes and eukaryotes, some of which have important functions within the cell.
