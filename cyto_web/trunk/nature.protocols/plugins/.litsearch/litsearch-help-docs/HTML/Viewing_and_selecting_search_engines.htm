Viewing and selecting search engines

 

You can view and select available search engines via the View -> Engine Selections  menu item.  When you do this, a dialog with checkboxes will pop up:

 

You can select or de-select a particular search engine by clicking in the checkbox next to it.    Note that, to comply with NCBI's and USPTO's system requirements for large queries, Agilent Literature Search places certain limits on its interaction with these search engines.  These limitations are listed along with the descriptions of the search engines below.

Search engines supported in this release of Agilent Literature Search are:

    •  PubMed, a service of the National Library of Medicine, includes over 15 million citations for biomedical articles back to the 1950's. These citations are from MEDLINE and additional life science journals. PubMed includes links to many sites providing full text articles and other related resources.   Limits: 3 second delay between every call to the PubMed engine (applies to both search and fetch), maximum 1000 documents processed per query.
    • OMIM,  Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man. This database is a catalog of human genes and genetic disorders developed for the World Wide Web by NCBI, the National Center for Biotechnology Information. The database contains textual information and references. It also contains copious links to MEDLINE and sequence records in the Entrez system, and links to additional related resources at NCBI and elsewhere.  (http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/entrez/query.fcgi?db=OMIM).  Limits: 3 second delay between every call to the OMIM engine (applies to both search and fetch), maximum 100 documents processed per query.
    • USPTO, United States Patent and Trademark Office (http://patft.uspto.gov).  Limits: maximum 50 documents per query term, 100 documents per query.
