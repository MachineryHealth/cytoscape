Cytoscape Home

 The 2nd Annual Cytoscape Retreat will be held September 8 and 9, 2004 (Wed-Thurs) at the Computational Biology Center at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, New York City.

Cytoscape is jointly developed by the groups of Benno Schwikowski (Pasteur Institute, Paris), Trey Ideker (University of California San Diego), Chris Sander (Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center) and Hamid Bolouri (Institute of Systems Biology, Seattle).

The meeting will mainly be informal and technically focused with all major Cytoscape team members gathered together to set new directions for Cytoscape, plan new features and collaboratively work on code. A number of short Cytoscape related research talks will be scheduled and a public Cytoscape tutorial will be given. Cytoscape related presentations by invitees are welcome. Social activities around the meeting are also scheduled.

Logistics
Location
The Cytoscape retreat will be held at the MSKCC Computational Biology Center (map, photos). It is located in the Upper East Side of Manhattan at 307 E. 63rd St. on the 2nd floor (between 1st and 2nd Aves.) The closest subway stations are 63rd/Lexington (F line) and 59th/Lexington (4,5,6,N,R,W lines). You need to buzz up to open the front door and then you will take the elevator to the 2nd floor.

Hotel
We have reserved a block of rooms at The Melrose Hotel (63rd St. and Lexington) for the nights of Sep.7, 8 and 9 (Wed, Thurs, Fri nights).  To book, call 1-800-MELROSE (in North America) or +212-838-5700 (international) and mention that you are part of the "Memorial Sloan" group for Sep 7-9.  You do not need to book all 3 nights, only book the nights you need. If you want to stay longer, the hotel can extend the bookings at the block rate if booked before Aug.7 based on hotel availability.

Rooms will cost $209/night (plus taxes - 8.625% sales tax, 5% city tax, $2 occupancy tax) if booked before Aug.7.2004. After that, the rooms at the Melrose will no longer be reserved and will be available at the regular rate of $279/night (plus taxes).  We urge you to book NOW to avoid higher costs and the chance of no vacancy.  The US Open Tennis Tournament is this week, so hotels are busy.  Check in time is 3pm and check out time is noon.

The Melrose Hotel is a 2 block walk to the Computational Biology Center (5-10 minutes).

The Melrose is the cheapest hotel within easy walking distance from MSKCC. Other hotels that may be cheaper can be booked on-line at Travelocity, Expedia or Hotels.com, for example. You can always take the subway ($2) or a taxi ($4-12) to MSKCC if you book a hotel farther away, although it will be less convenient. Just make sure it is in Manhattan (preferably between downtown and 96th St.) and not other parts of the city (e.g. Queens, Brooklyn, Bronx or Staten Island) as these locations are generally much less convenient.

Regular room rates for hotels in Manhattan at any time of year are at least $150/night, although there are frequently specials that can be obtained on-line.

All costs mentioned here are in US Dollars.
